# My love of The Goblin, explained
I have long held a fascination with goblins. Both within and without of my current favorite game (Magic the Gathering), I'm drawn to the chaotic nature of little green troublemakers.

![Art from 6th Edition Goblin King](https://cards.scryfall.io/art_crop/front/4/4/440546d3-91d4-45f3-ae36-bdf5bec2b673.jpg?1691495707)

For the longest time, my online screen name (which I will not specifically mention here!) has been a direct reference to a famous goblin from Dungeons & Dragons. Within that specific game and the overarching lore, they are often considered mere cannon fodder - or worse, pinatas that contain experience for players to vacuum up through ruthless extermination.

I've been in a continuously running, biweekly group of Dungeons & Dragons for about six years now. In those games, I made it a point to always approach goblins diplomatically. It's become something of a running joke in our group that I will never attack first when it comes to the little green guys. This works more often than not, as my DM is a huge fan of non-violent problem solving in D&D, and I almost always play a charisma based character.

Why do I like these little guys? It's hard to put a finger on the exact reason, but if I had to catalogue them:
- Mischevious
- Scrappy
- Always up to some nonsense when way worse things are happening in the world
- Somehow able to succeed, despite impossible odds

This feels like a pretty good descriptor for a positive interpretation of goblins, though I know many look at them as vermin in the various multiverses in which they exist. This feels unfair to me, as they're more akin to an unwitting clown group than an active threat most times they show up. 

![The Wandering Inn, No Killing Goblins Sign](https://i.etsystatic.com/59708915/r/il/b7cdae/7078056838/il_570xN.7078056838_3u49.jpg)
If you want to see a great interpretation of goblins (and the problematic nature of casting them as a group to be genocided), check out The Wandering Inn. The series started as a web novel, and has been adapted into (at the time of writing) 17 audiobooks each about 30 hours long. There is a ton of goblin content, and their plight is handled with the dignity it deserves.

This post doesn't have a point, exactly, I just wanted to create a blog post about something I feel strongly about. Goblins are good, not evil. Treat goblins with respect. They might just bring you a trinket!
